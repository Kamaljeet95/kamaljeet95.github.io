Research Interest

Strongly interacting matter from ALICE data to QCD transport

The research program connects experimental heavy-ion measurements with phenomenological studies of transport in magnetized QCD matter and interdisciplinary accelerator-material design.

ϕ meson production in heavy-ion collisions

Current work focuses on the production of the ϕ meson in Pb–Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.36 TeV using Run–3 ALICE data in the forward-rapidity dimuon channel. The dimuon decay mode provides a clean signature with reduced sensitivity to final-state interactions.

Spin alignment and pp reference studies

Alongside the Pb–Pb program, the work includes ϕ-meson production and polarization in pp collisions at √s = 13.6 TeV, with emphasis on signal extraction, angular-decay analysis, and cross-section systematic studies.

Thermoelectric QCD matter

Phenomenological work studies electrical and thermal conductivity, induced electromagnetic fields, Wiedemann–Franz behavior, Thomson effects, Soret and Dufour effects, and the thermoelectric figure of merit across the QCD phase transition.

Future direction

A long-term goal is to build a unified framework for thermoelectric transport across QCD matter and condensed-matter systems, translating microscopic transport insight into design principles for advanced thermoelectric and accelerator materials.
